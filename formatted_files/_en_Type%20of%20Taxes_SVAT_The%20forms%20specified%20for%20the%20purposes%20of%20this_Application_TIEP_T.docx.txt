<span>Income Tax (IT)</span></div><div><h3>Income Tax (IT)</h3><p>Income tax is charged on the basis of the provisions under the Inland Revenue Act, No. 24 of 2017 as  amended by the Inland Revenue (Amendment) Act, No. 10 of 2021. The Inland Revenue Act provides the legal authority to charge, levy and collect income tax on the gains and profits of every person, which arose or is arising to such person for every year of assessment commencing after 1<sup>st</sup> of April, 2018. </p><h5>Sources of Income :</h5><ul><li><strong>Employment Income </strong></li><ul><li>Calculation of an individual's gains and profits from employment for a year of assessment </li></ul><li><strong>Business Income</strong></li><ul><li>A person's income from a business for a year of assessment is the person's gains and profits from conducting the business for the year. </li></ul><li><strong>Investment Income</strong></li><ul><li>A person's income from an investment for a year of assessment is the person's gains and profits from conducting the investment for the year.</li></ul><li><strong>Other Income </strong></li><ul><li>A person’s income from other sources for a year of assessment is the person’s gains and profits from any source of any kind, however does not include profits of a casual and non-recurring nature. </li></ul><p>In respect of a person who is deemed to be resident in Sri Lanka is chargeable with income tax in respect of his income from Sri Lanka and income derived by him from outside Sri Lanka. The liability to income tax therefore extends to his global income. A person who is deemed to be non-resident in Sri Lanka is chargeable with income tax in respect of only the gains and profits, arising or derived from Sri Lanka.​​</p><p>An individual,  who is a
<span>resident in Sri Lanka</span> for a year of assessment or who is a
<span>non- resident in Sri Lanka</span> for a year of assessment,
<span>but is a citizen of Sri Lanka</span>, will receive an aggregate relief of; </p><ol type="i"><li>Rs. 500,000, for each year of assessment prior to January 1, 2020;  </li><li>Rs. 3,000,000, for each year of assessment commencing on or after January 1, 2020</li></ol><p>except that an individual who is a trustee, receiver, executor or liquidator shall not be entitled to deduct this personal relief as such trustee, receiver, executor or liquidator, and the relief shall not be deducted against gains from the realisation of investment assets.​​</p></ul></div><div><h4>Acts</h4><table><tbody><tr><td><a href="https://www.ird.gov.lk/en/publications/Acts_Income Tax_2017/IR_Act_No_24_2017_E.pdf">Inland Revenue Act, No.24 of 2017
